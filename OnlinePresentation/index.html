Literature Review: Computational Foundations

Vehicle Routing Problem with Time Windows (VRPTW)

  • Seminal Work: Solomon (1987) established foundational heuristics and benchmark instances for the VRPTW. [Solomon, 1987]
  • Exact Methods: Desrochers et al. (1992) introduced column generation for provably optimal solutions on small-to-medium instances. [Desrochers et al., 1992]
  • NP-Hardness: Real-world q-commerce scale (high order volumes, stochastic demand) makes exact methods computationally prohibitive. [Toth & Vigo, 2002]
  • Modern Approaches: Metaheuristics (GA, Tabu Search) and Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) enable sub-second dispatch decisions.

Literature Review: Safety Routing & Behavioral Logistics

Safety-Aware and Risk-Aware Routing

  • Hazmat Origins: Early safety models minimised population exposure in hazardous materials logistics. [Zografos & Androutsopoulos, 2004]
  • Urban Safety Index: Hoseinzadeh et al. (2020) defined a composite index using historical crash data and real-time driver volatility telematics. [Hoseinzadeh et al., 2020]
  • RADR Framework: Spatio-temporal graph learning proactively avoids high-risk segments, reducing risk exposure by ~18% with minimal time impact (~2%).
  • FMEA Risk Model: $R_{ij} = P_{ij} \times C_{ij}$ — probability of adverse event multiplied by projected impact.

Behavioral Logistics — The Human Element

  • Income-Targeting: Gig riders exhibit non-rational patterns where financial goals dictate risk tolerance. [Sinchaisri et al., 2023]
  • Systemic Failure: Q-commerce safety incidents result from algorithmic pressure inducing structural risk-taking. [Salmon et al., 2023]
  • Nudging Strategies: Information nudging (real-time crash probabilities) and monetary incentives for choosing safer routes. [Short Summary, 2024]
  • Piece-Rate Wages: Payment per delivery strongly associated with speeding, fatigue, and poor safety outcomes.
Key Insight: Algorithms directly increase systemic safety risk. The "double-edged sword" of performance-based pay promotes unsafe driving as riders maximise trip counts per shift.

Identified Research Gaps

  1. Technological Integration Scale: Engineering challenge of integrating national-level crash databases (iRAD) into sub-second dispatch loops is under-explored.
  2. Longitudinal Fatigue Modelling: Behavioral models are snapshots; they do not account for cumulative fatigue over 12-hour gig-economy shifts.
  3. Governance Trade-offs: No established standard for acceptable "time penalty" in exchange for safety from a regulatory perspective.
  4. Stakeholder Conflict Resolution: No mathematical models for resolving conflict between platform profit, customer time, and rider safety.
Thesis Contribution: A unified SA-VRPTW framework that treats rider safety not as a constraint, but as a primary Pareto objective of urban logistics.

SA-VRPTW Mathematical Formulation

The framework uses Soft Time Windows (STW) and probabilistic risk modelling, defining a true multi-objective Pareto optimisation front.

Network and Parameters

  • Directed graph $G = (V, E)$ where $V = \{0\} \cup C$ (depot + $N$ customers)
  • Homogeneous fleet $K$ with capacity $Q = 2$ (q-commerce micro-batching)
  • Soft Time Windows $[e_i, l_i]$ with exponential lateness penalty:
Lateness Penalty
$$P_L(\tau_i^k) = e^{0.12\,\tau_i^k} - 1$$
  • Edge attributes: $t_{ij}$ (travel time), $r_{ij} \in [0,1]$ (crash probability), $c_{ij} \in [0,1]$ (congestion), $h_{ij} \in \{0,1\}$ (residential flag)

Pareto Objective Functions

Objective 1 — Efficiency & Soft Penalties
$$\min F_1 = \sum_{k \in K} \sum_{(i,j) \in E} t_{ij}\, x_{ij}^k + \sum_{k \in K} \sum_{i \in C} P_L(\tau_i^k)$$
Objective 2 — Probabilistic Route Survival
$$\min F_2 = \sum_{k \in K} \sum_{(i,j) \in E} -\ln(1 - r_{ij})\, x_{ij}^k$$

Rather than summing arbitrary risk indices, we maximise the absolute probability of accident-free route completion via log-survival transformation.

Source: SafetyAwareRoutingProposal.tex, Section 4

Novel Cyber-Physical Constraints

Behavioral Rider Fatigue Threshold ($S_{max}$)

Riders suffer cognitive degradation under sustained high-stress corridor exposure. We enforce a maximum cumulative stress budget per route:

$$\sum_{(i,j) \in E} -\ln(1 - r_{ij})\, x_{ij}^k \leq S_{max}, \quad \forall k \in K$$

where $S_{max} = 5.0$ represents the empirically calibrated fatigue ceiling.

Vulnerable Neighbourhood Protection ($H_{cap}$)

To prevent routing delivery traffic through school zones and residential areas to avoid major intersections:

$$\sum_{k \in K} \sum_{(i,j) \in E} h_{ij}\, x_{ij}^k \leq H_{cap}$$

where $H_{cap} = 8$ limits residential road usage across the fleet.

Standard Routing Constraints

  • Visit Uniqueness: Each customer visited exactly once
  • Flow Conservation: Vehicles entering a node must leave it
  • Depot Return: Each route starts and ends at assigned depot
  • Capacity: Total load per route $\leq Q$
  • Subtour Elimination: Miller-Tucker-Zemlin (MTZ) formulation

Solution Approaches

1. Exact Methods — MILP

  • Mixed-Integer Linear Programming via PuLP + CBC solver
  • Provides provably optimal baseline for academic validation
  • Computational intractability beyond $N = 20$ nodes
  • Implements $S_{max}$ and $H_{cap}$ constraints per rider

Source: code/08_run_real_benchmarks.py — solve_milp_advanced()

2. Genetic Algorithm (GA)

  • Population size: 50, Generations: 50–100
  • Elitist selection with tournament ($k=3$)
  • Order Crossover (OX) + Swap Mutation
  • Giant-tour split procedure for route partitioning
  • Fitness: $\lambda_1 T + \lambda_2 R + \lambda_3 C + \text{penalty}$

Source: code/07_solver_ga.py

3. Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL)

  • Pointer Network architecture trained via REINFORCE algorithm
  • $O(1)$ constant-time inference once deployed
  • 8–12% optimality gap vs. exact MILP solution

Conclusion

Key Contributions

  1. SA-VRPTW-STW Framework: A rigorous multi-objective Pareto optimisation formulation that elevates rider safety from a soft constraint to a primary objective.
  2. Log-Survival Risk Model: Probabilistic route survival ($-\ln(1 - r_{ij})$) replaces arbitrary additive risk indices, ensuring unit-consistent optimisation.
  3. Novel Constraints: $S_{max}$ (Behavioral Fatigue Threshold) and $H_{cap}$ (Neighbourhood Protection) introduce cyber-physical safeguards absent in prior work.
  4. Empirical Validation: Cross-city benchmarks on 6 Indian metropolises using authentic OSM topologies and iRAD-modelled crash data.
  5. Multi-Solver Evaluation: Comparative analysis of MILP, GA, and DRL demonstrating operational trade-offs between solution quality and inference speed.

Summary of Findings

  • SA-VRPTW routes are 15–20% longer but 40% safer in collision probability.
  • DRL achieves 8–12% optimality gap with constant-time inference.
  • Exponential STW penalties mathematically demonstrate that rigid 10-minute SLAs incentivise unsafe riding.
  • $S_{max}$ constraint prevents cognitive stress threshold breach during extended delivery shifts.
  • Risk profiles vary dramatically across cities — topology-aware algorithm selection is essential.
Thesis Statement: Balancing rapid delivery constraints against rider survivability is mathematically achievable and operationally scalable through advanced metaheuristics and neural augmentation.

Future Work and Extensions

1. Stochastic Traffic and Risk Prediction

Migrate from static probabilities to predictive Graph Neural Networks (GNN) capable of anticipating flash-congestion and spatial risk propagation dynamically across connected civic edges.

2. Active Behavioral Telematics

Formulate an active-learning pipeline where continuous smartphone gyroscope and biometric outputs of riders directly update the cognitive fatigue threshold $S_{max}$ in real-time, autonomously halting dispatch cadence before physiological collapse.

3. Continuous Micro-Batching Logic

Transform standard node-based constraint logic into a continuous Dynamic Pickup and Delivery Problem (DPDP), accurately mapping the fragmented, minimal-item micro-capacity batching mechanisms of modern q-commerce dark stores.

4. SUMO Traffic Simulation

Integrate with the Simulation of Urban Mobility (SUMO) platform for high-fidelity microscopic traffic simulation, validating routing decisions against realistic traffic signal timing and vehicle interactions.

Planned: code/11_sumo_integration.py

5. Regulatory and Policy Integration

  • Alignment with Rajasthan Platform Based Gig Workers Act 2023 and Karnataka Draft Bill 2024
  • Dynamic SLAs that automatically extend delivery windows during rain or high-congestion periods
  • Explainable AI (SHAP) for algorithmic transparency in rider-facing applications

6. Real iRAD Data Access

Securing formal access rights from MoRTH for validated crash data to replace synthetic modelling with authentic government records.
